## Why They Stalled
- Too many concurrent objectives reduced iteration depth per objective.
- Problem framing evolved faster than stable metric pipelines.
- Novelty pressure came before baseline maturity.

## How To Use These Failures in an M.Tech Thesis
1. Treat each failed direction as a negative result with explicit hypothesis and rejection reason.
2. Document what was held constant, what was varied, and why conclusions were credible.
3. Show decision logic for pivoting to WOSAC benchmark-first strategy.
4. Include concrete lessons that improved final methodology.

## Suggested Thesis Section Structure
- Early exploration phase (broad hypotheses).
- Failure analysis (methodological and empirical).
- Pivot rationale (benchmark clarity, measurable baselines).
- Final focused track (WOSAC) with reproducible outcomes.

## Evidence Checklist
- Commit references for each abandoned direction.
- At least one metric snapshot or artifact per direction.
- One paragraph on what each failure prevented you from misunderstanding later.

## Rule
A failed direction counts as thesis contribution only if the stopping criterion and evidence are explicit.
